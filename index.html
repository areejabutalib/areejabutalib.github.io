About

Myself

Welcome to my personal portfolio. I’m Areej Abutaleb, a recent MSc Clinical Neuroscience graduate from City, University of London. I studied Psychology for my undergrad, and during that time my interest in human behaviour expanded. In particular, I wanted to explore more about the specific neural underpinnings that can govern our perception, thoughts, and behaviour about ourselves and others. This curiosity encouraged me to further study Neuroscience for my Masters. My final thesis involved the study of how we can adopt different learning strategies to influence our decision-making, generating important implications towards neuroeconomics and consumer behaviour.

Journey to Coding

My interest in coding really began during my masters whilst completing a project as part of one of my modules, where we used MATLAB to perform statistical procedures such as computational modelling, data analysis, and coding experiments. I thoroughly enjoyed witnessing the process of starting from one line of code, and, several lines of code later, possessing a fully functional and responsive program on the computer screen. Soon after, I decided to venture out and that was when I signed up and took part in Code First: Girls’ 8-week ‘Intro to Web Development’ course, an organisation that aims to introduce more women to the world of tech. Throughout the course, we were taught the fundamentals of HTML, CSS, and JavaScript. We concluded the course by splitting up into teams and producing a website of our choice, using wireframes to plan out the basic structure and visual representation of our website. My team and I chose to present a mock hotel website where you can book your stay in a room of your choice. You can view more details about the website on my GitHub, linked below in the 'Projects' section.

After completion of the course I delved a lot more deeper into Responsive Web Development, and coded this portfolio using HTML, CSS, JavaScript, and jQuery. I hope to utilise the skills and research methods I have acquired throughout my studies to work in User Experience (UX) research and contribute towards the success of companies through optimising users' insights and experiences of various products.

MIND Recovery Hub Intern

Nov 2019 - Present

MIND in the City, Hackney, and Waltham Forest

I undertook a clinical internship with MIND as part of my Masters, where I worked for MIND charity in their Recovery Hub. The scope of my internship involved supporting clients with mental health needs by co-facilitaing and planning various group sessions as part of the Wellbeing Network Recovery Pathway. I was also involved in admin work such as using Salesforce to update clients' progress notes, and phoning clients to brief them on Recovery Hub services.

Graduate Research Assistant (Neuroscience)

Nov 2019 - Oct 2020

City, University of London

Cognitive Neuroscience Research Unit

I worked as a Graduate Research Assistant in City University's Cognitive Neuroscience Research Unit (CNRU), where I assisted on a research study that aimed to explore learning strategies implemented during decision-making, and how these adaptive learning strategies affect our choices when faced with uncertain situations. I was involved in recruiting and briefing participants through a computerised task that aimed to investigate which learning strategy subjects adopt through their pattern of choices.

Ecommerce Merchandising Allocator

Aug 2018 - April 2019

River Island Head Office

Straight after I received my Psychology degree, I worked in the Ecommerce sector at River Island's Head Office in London, where I was an Ecommerce Allocator and was mainly responsible for store stock replenishment. Microsoft Excel was our main reporting system, which we used to produce weekly reports and insights on product performance, including formulas and functions such as VLOOKUP, COUNTIF, and Pivot Tables. I also liaised and communicated with the other departments to ensure website merchandising and promotional efforts were effectively allocated in order to maximise sales and branding objectives.

Undergraduate Research Assistant (Cognitive Neuroscience)

Oct 2016 - May 2017

Middlesex University

As part of my placement year during my undergrad, I worked as a Research Assistant in Cognitive Neuroscience at Middlesex University. I collaborated with two research supervisors and assisted them with their projects. I was responsible for recruiting participants through an online recruiting system (SONA), and administered neuropsychological assessments including Weschsler Adult Intelligence Scale (WAIS) and the Weschsler Memory Scale (WMS). I was also required to set up and administer EEG to monitor participants' brain activity during computerised tasks. I carried out literature searches and drafted literature reviews, and presented data findings to faculty members and students at our Psychology Placement Conference.
